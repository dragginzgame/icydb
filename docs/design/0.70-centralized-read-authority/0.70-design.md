## Title

**0.70 — Centralized Read Authority Classification**

---

## Status

In Progress

---

## Context

The `0.69` line introduced **true covering read execution** and established
that certain SQL query cohorts can execute correctly under staleness **without
per-row existence probes**, when backed by appropriate authority guarantees
(e.g. `storage_existence_witness`).

Subsequent iterations admitted multiple **specific cohorts** where:

* query semantics remained identical to the fail-closed baseline
* probe and encoding overhead dropped to zero
* execution became measurably cheaper

However, admission of these cohorts is currently:

* **implicit**
* **distributed across multiple modules** (`scan`, `covering`, `terminal`)
* **not centrally defined**

This makes it difficult to:

* predict whether a change applies broadly or narrowly
* reason about correctness boundaries
* extend the system safely

The next `0.70` constraint is now explicit:

* even a proven probe-free cohort must fail closed unless the backing index is
  explicitly query-visible as `Valid`

---

## Problem

Authority decisions (probe vs probe-free execution) are currently:

* encoded indirectly through execution paths
* duplicated across multiple layers
* derived from mechanism rather than principle

As a result:

* behavior appears empirical (“this cohort worked”)
* correctness boundaries are hard to audit
* future extensions risk unsound widening

The most important lesson from the later `0.69.x` widenings is that the risk
is not random route drift. It is **shape intuition outrunning the actual
authority invariant**.

Several cohorts that looked syntactically adjacent behaved differently once
stale rows were introduced. Others that were initially suspected to be unsafe
later proved admissible once the shared kernel preserved the right
membership-level witness end to end.

---

## Key Observation (from 0.69)

Empirical results across admitted and rejected cohorts reveal a unifying
invariant:

> Probe-free admission is not determined by syntactic shape categories.
> It is determined by whether the query's final bounded window is **stable
> under deletion of matching rows**.

For cohorts that satisfy that invariant:

* row-existence probes are unnecessary
* covering-membership or storage witness is sufficient

For cohorts that do not satisfy it, or that have not been proven to satisfy
it:

* missing rows can change the output window
* probes remain required

---

## Core Invariant

A query may execute without row-existence probes iff:

> **Removing any matching row does not change the final TOP-N result produced by the query.**

This property is referred to as:

> **window stability under deletion**

This property cannot be reliably inferred from surface query shape alone.

It must instead be:

* **derived conservatively** from an explicit invariant the planner/runtime can
  actually justify, or
* **validated empirically** and then admitted explicitly as a proven cohort

That is the main `0.69.x` correction. Early shape-based intuition was useful
for finding candidates, but it was not strong enough to serve as the admission
rule.

---

## Current 0.70 Scope

The currently implemented `0.70` invariant is intentionally narrow:

> **Probe-free covering authority requires `IndexState::Valid`.**

That means:

* `WitnessValidated` routes require a `Valid` index
* `StorageExistenceWitness` routes require a `Valid` index
* `Building` and `Dropping` indexes fail closed back to probe-based execution

This is the honest boundary of the current slice.

It does **not** yet claim that:

* every index-backed shortcut requires `Valid`
* aggregate `COUNT` / `EXISTS` existing-row shortcuts have been classified
  under the same validity model
* all index-backed execution contracts already consume one centralized
  validity-aware authority classifier

So the current statement is intentionally:

* "`Valid` gates probe-free covering authority"

and not:

* "`Valid` gates every index-backed authority shortcut"

Index validity therefore has one explicit `0.70` meaning:

* `IndexState::Valid` is currently required for all probe-free covering
  authority paths

Other index-backed execution paths, including aggregate shortcuts, are
explicitly out of scope for this design slice and may require separate or
extended validity rules later.

The external inspection surface is now explicit too for the current
classifier-owned covering families:

* store-backed `EXPLAIN EXECUTION` for classifier-owned covering routes surfaces
  `index_state`
* it also surfaces the final `authority_decision`
* and it surfaces one narrow `authority_reason` such as
  `synchronized_pair_witness`, `stale_storage_existence_witness`, or
  `index_not_valid`
* the same flat authority classification is also locked on the canonical
  execution-descriptor JSON payload used by structured execution explain
  consumers

The current mapping is intentionally flat and stable:

* `authority_decision`
  * `row_check_required`
  * `witness_validated`
  * `storage_existence_witness`
* `authority_reason`
  * `index_not_valid`
  * `synchronized_pair_witness`
  * `stale_storage_existence_witness`
  * `authoritative_witness_unavailable`
  * `probe_required`

For the current classifier-owned store-backed secondary covering inspection surface:

* `authority_decision` + `authority_reason` together represent the authority
  classification
* `existing_row_mode` remains on the covering node as the covering-specific
  execution contract
* no extra normalization layer is introduced yet

Resolved-profile-only covering routes still keep their covering-specific
`existing_row_mode`, but they do not yet emit the flat classifier authority
labels.

That keeps `0.70` authority classification debuggable from outside the
executor instead of requiring callers to reverse-engineer probe-free
promotion from internal route state alone.

The current `0.70.3` implementation is intentionally narrow:

* one canonical `ResolvedSecondaryReadAuthorityProfile` is now the production
  source of truth for store-backed secondary read authority
* that resolved profile always carries:
  * `index_state`
  * `existing_row_mode`
  * `flat_classifier: Option<...>`
* one direct `secondary_covering_probe_free_mode_for_store(...)` helper now
  resolves the richer covering-specific probe-free mode before any flat
  projection is considered
* the optional flat projection currently covers:
  * the centralized single-component store-backed authority line
  * one explicit composite synchronized-witness family:
    * composite order-only covering under the stronger synchronized pair
      witness
* the same resolved profile now feeds:
  * route promotion
  * the matching single-component non-covering `EXPLAIN EXECUTION` surface
  * the current classifier-owned covering `EXPLAIN EXECUTION` labels for:
    * order-only covering
    * equality-prefix primary-key order
    * bounded strict-text range
    * expression key-only order/range siblings
    * synchronized composite order-only covering
* the downgraded single-component stale path reuses the same flat
  `authority_decision=row_check_required` +
  `authority_reason=authoritative_witness_unavailable` contract across core,
  generated parity, and PocketIC
* stale composite covering and the broader composite / multi-component line
  still remain resolved-profile-only for now:
  * they keep their covering-specific `existing_row_mode`
  * they do not inherit flat classifier labels
  * any future widening has to change that admission boundary deliberately
    instead of routing composite plans through the flat projection by
    resemblance

### 0.70.3 Ground Truth

At the current `0.70.3` stop-point, production has one read-authority object
and one optional compatibility projection.

The canonical production object is:

* `ResolvedSecondaryReadAuthorityProfile`

It always carries:

* `index_state`
* `existing_row_mode`
* `flat_classifier: Option<...>`

The flat projection currently exists only for:

* the centralized single-component secondary line
* the one explicitly proven composite synchronized-witness family

Its vocabulary is intentionally frozen to those currently admitted
probe-free profiles. It must not widen just because a new route looks
structurally similar.

Resolved-profile-only routes still include:

* stale/storage-witness composite families
* broader richer structural covering cases

The external surface follows the same split:

* resolved profiles with an admitted flat classifier projection emit flat
  `authority_decision` + `authority_reason`
* resolved-profile-only routes keep their covering-specific `existing_row_mode`
  without inheriting those flat labels

So the current stop-point is not accidental drift. It is the explicit
temporary boundary of `0.70.3`.

The older owner vocabulary and the older internal second-profile helper have
both been removed entirely. The explicit boundary is now just:

* rich behavior always exists
* flat classifier projection exists only when admitted

### Classifier Projection Admissibility

The flat classifier is a projection of the resolved profile, not the
other way around.

A profile is eligible for flat classifier projection only when its authority
story is simple enough to survive that lossy projection. The current bar is:

* no stale dependency beyond the already-admitted flat vocabulary
* deterministic authority outcome for the full route
* single-pass execution without extra phase-specific validation
* no richer structural distinction that execution still needs after routing

If a cohort still depends on richer witness structure, stale-only semantics,
or multi-phase validation, it should remain resolved-profile-only even if it shares
some syntax with a classifier-owned family.

What `0.70.3` still did **not** do:

* it did not generalize to all composite covering routes
* it did not pull stale composite/storage-witness cases into the flat
  classifier
* it did not widen planner policy

Any later expansion should therefore start from this exact serialized state,
not from intuition about nearby cohorts.

## Empirical Widening Note

Early `0.69` work suggested that equality-prefix leading-component cohorts were
unsafe as a class.

Subsequent `0.69.x` validation showed that this was too coarse:

* at least one descending leading-component cohort proved admissible once the
  shared kernel stopped dropping membership-level witness data
* that admission came from preserving the right witness through the shared
  kernel, not from the cohort merely resembling an already-admitted shape

So the classifier lesson is now explicit:

* do **not** start from "this syntactic family seems safe"
* do start from "this cohort is proven safe under the invariant"

The classifier should therefore grow from **evidence-backed cohorts**, not
from aggressive shape generalization.

## Implemented 0.70.3 Architecture

Production now centralizes store-backed secondary read authority in:

```text
crates/icydb-core/src/db/executor/authority/read.rs
```

That module owns one canonical production object:

```rust
ResolvedSecondaryReadAuthorityProfile {
    index_state,
    existing_row_mode,
    flat_classifier: Option<...>,
}
```

The ordering is deliberate:

1. resolve rich behavior first
2. derive the flat classifier projection only if the profile is admissible

That means:

* route behavior keys off `existing_row_mode`
* `EXPLAIN` flat labels are emitted only when `flat_classifier` exists
* the flat classifier is not allowed to inspect storage, schema, or route
* the flat classifier is not allowed to become a second correctness seam

The current admitted flat-projection families are:

* the centralized single-component secondary line
* one explicitly proven synchronized-witness composite order-only family

Everything else remains resolved-profile-only.

---

## Non-Goals

This design explicitly does NOT:

* redesign the execution engine
* unify scan or covering implementations
* generalize to all SQL shapes
* support OFFSET or cursor-based continuation
* expand grouped or aggregate execution
* claim that aggregate `COUNT` / `EXISTS` index shortcuts are already covered
  by the same validity rule

The next aggregate pass, if taken later, should be treated as a separate
classification problem around aggregate-result sensitivity to missing rows,
not as one automatic extension of probe-free covering admission.

---

## Testing Strategy

### 1. Projection invariants

* resolved profile round-trips the current flat classifier vocabulary
* flat classifier projection remains intentionally lossy relative to the
  resolved profile
* an admitted flat projection must never downgrade an already probe-free mode

---

### 2. Route and explain consistency

* route promotion must consume the resolved profile directly
* flat `authority_decision` / `authority_reason` labels must come from the
  resolved profile projection only
* stale composite/storage-witness routes must remain resolved-profile-only and
  must not inherit flat labels by accident

---

### 3. Parity validation

* classifier-owned routes keep the same explain/result behavior across:
  * core tests
  * generated SQL parity
  * PocketIC
* resolved-profile-only routes keep their covering-specific
  `existing_row_mode` contract without accidentally emitting flat labels

---

## Expected Impact

### Immediate

* explicit and auditable authority decisions
* reduced cognitive load when evaluating changes
* elimination of “why did this work?” ambiguity
* less temptation to widen by syntactic resemblance alone

### Medium-term

* simpler extension of new cohorts
* reduced duplication across execution paths
* clearer separation between:

  * **invariant / evidence-based admission reasoning**
  * **execution mechanics**

---

## Relationship to 0.69

0.69 established:

> “probe-free execution is possible for some covering read shapes”

0.70 formalizes:

> **“which cohorts qualify under a proven invariant, and why”**

This is a continuation, not a divergence.

---

## Future Work

* keep widening only where a resolved-profile cohort can clear the explicit
  flat-projection admissibility gate
* extend flat projection to additional validated cohorts only after their
  richer witness story no longer matters to execution
* integrate grouped and aggregate lanes later as a separate missing-row
  sensitivity problem
* keep unifying authority reasoning across read execution paths without
  reintroducing classifier-driven correctness branching

---

## Summary

0.70 now centralizes store-backed secondary read authority around one
canonical resolved profile:

* rich behavior is resolved first
* flat classifier projection is optional and explicitly admissible
* probe-free covering still requires `IndexState::Valid`
* stale/storage-witness and richer structural cases stay outside the flat
  projection until a broader invariant is proven

That keeps probe-free read execution evolvable without letting shape-based
intuition or legacy classifier vocabulary silently become authority.
