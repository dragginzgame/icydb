## Slice 5 Result

The accepted entity snapshot now persists bounded activation identity, origin,
kind, state, base-schema fingerprint, epoch, and semantic fingerprint under the
sole current version-1 `ICYY` profile. Reserved IDs and names survive
Enforcing-to-Validating-to-validated promotion; abort removes the activation
while leaving allocator high-water unchanged so the identity cannot be reused.

`SchemaStore` owns the exact activation/job biconditional: an
`EnforcingNewWrites` activation has no job, a `Validating` activation has one
matching job, and orphan, missing, mismatched, or malformed jobs fail closed.
Accepted publication also freezes every other fact for an entity with a live
activation and permits only one exact lifecycle transition. Recovery consumes
that same transition and closure authority rather than reconstructing it.

Journaled stores persist one bounded `ICJA` Forward/Verify job. Pages scan in
canonical primary-key order under row, decoded-byte, and finding bounds. A
finding receipt is replayed byte-for-byte until its monotonic sequence is
acknowledged. Clean Forward exhaustion captures the store's durable
data-mutation revision; Verify checks it before and after the sweep and restarts
Forward on drift. Schema and job journal records do not advance that row-only
revision.

Activation start, job advancement, promotion, and abort use marker-owned
journal records. Promotion moves the exact accepted check into the validated
catalog and deletes the job in the same marker; abort deletes the activation
and job while retiring its identity. Heap stores may promote only after one
bounded exact sweep proves exhaustion; otherwise they reject rather than claim
multi-message stability without durable revision authority.

Focused evidence covers current activation/job codecs and malformed forms,
exact activation/job closure, ID preservation and retirement, receipt
acknowledgement/replay, row-only revision stability, empty Forward/Verify
promotion, historical findings, same-entity schema freeze, and recovery from
interruption before or after activation/job marker append. The repository-wide
suite remains human-owned under `AGENTS.md`.

## Slice 6B Result

Generated unique additions now reserve one planner-invisible candidate index
whose nonzero physical generation is bound to the activation epoch. One
candidate projection compiles the accepted key and optional predicate contract;
the bounded Forward runner derives that projection once per row, detects
same-page and previously staged duplicate keys, and publishes candidate entries
with the durable job checkpoint. Verify is read-only and promotes only after an
unchanged source revision and an exact complete candidate generation.

The shared accepted write program blocks inserts, replacements, and updates
that author candidate key or predicate-dependency slots while the generation is
incomplete. Unrelated updates remain admissible, and commit preparation removes
the exact staged key for a source delete. The same barrier therefore covers
typed, structural, SQL, batch, and bulk mutation paths without a frontend-owned
unique policy.

Candidate keys remain derived state: the journal persists the exact validation
checkpoint, while recovery clears index storage and reconstructs only the
Forward prefix authorized by that checkpoint, or the complete generation in
Verify. Recovery now discards the volatile schema overlay before canonical tail
folding so a sequence of activation and job records validates against its true
canonical predecessor rather than a newer live state.

Promotion atomically moves the candidate definition into the accepted index
set, preserves its physical generation, installs the reserved unique
constraint, and removes the validation job. Duplicate findings remain bounded
and replayable and cannot expose the candidate to planning. Focused evidence
covers accepted transition classification, codec round trips, clean and
duplicate staging, typed insert rejection, source-delete maintenance, stable
promotion, and interruption after marker publication.
